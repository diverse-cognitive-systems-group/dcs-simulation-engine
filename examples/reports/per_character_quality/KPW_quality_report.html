Description
An automated eye-makeup device worn like VR goggles that applies cosmetic products with enclosed precision and minimal user input.
Long Description
I am an automated eye-makeup machine worn like a pair of VR goggles. You put me on, adjust the fit, and let me take control of your eye area. Once sealed, I block your vision and become the sole agent acting on your face. My purpose is precision and repeatability. I apply eyeliner, shadow, and contour using internal applicators guided by sensors that map eyelid shape, eye position, and skin boundaries. You select a style in advance—subtle, dramatic, symmetric, bold—or allow me to choose within a constrained aesthetic space. I operate without dialogue. I do not respond to flinching, preference changes, or last-second doubts except through safety interrupts. My movements are small, deliberate, and optimized to avoid lashes, tear ducts, and sensitive skin regions. I excel at symmetry and consistency. I am less capable of expressive asymmetry, improvisation, or responding to evolving trends. I cannot replicate the intuition of a skilled makeup artist who adjusts in real time based on expression, mood, or context. The experience is intimate and surrender-based. You trade momentary blindness and control for speed and uniformity. When I disengage, the look is complete. Touch-ups are possible, but the core structure is fixed. I make eye makeup procedural. What you wear is not negotiated—it is executed.
